Why "Just 50%" isn't enough

The classic rule says spend 50% on needs. But if you live in London, New York, or have a high mortgage, your housing cost alone might eat up 40-50% of your income. This calculator adjusts to your reality.

Scenario 1: The "House Poor" Trap

If your rent is more than 35-40% of your income, you have very little left for groceries and utilities within the "Needs" bucket. In this case, you must borrow from your "Wants" (30%) category to survive.

Scenario 2: The "Parents" Advantage

If you live rent-free, you have a massive advantage. Don't spend that extra 50% on "Wants." Instead, flip the rule: Save 50%, Spend 30%, Need 20%. This allows you to build a house deposit in record time.

3 Pro Tips to Hack Your Budget

  • Pay Yourself First: Set up an automatic transfer for your 20% savings the day you get paid. Don't save what is left after spending; spend what is left after saving.
  • The 72-Hour Rule: Want to buy something expensive? Wait 72 hours. 80% of the time, the urge to buy will fade.
  • Audit Your Subscriptions: Are you paying for a gym you never visit or streaming services you don't watch? Cancel them.

What if my numbers don't fit?

Real life is messy. If your "Needs" are over 50%, you have two options:

  • Increase Income: Side hustles, asking for a raise, or changing jobs.
  • Decrease Fixed Costs: Move to a cheaper apartment, get a roommate, or sell a car with high payments.

Remember, this rule is a guideline, not a law. The most important step is simply starting to track where your money goes.
