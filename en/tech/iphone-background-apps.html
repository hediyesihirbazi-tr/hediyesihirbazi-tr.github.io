Stop Closing Background Apps on iPhone! (Here is Why)

It‚Äôs a habit we all have. You unlock your iPhone, double-swipe up, and compulsively close every single app in the background. It feels like cleaning your room. It feels satisfying. But according to Apple executives, you are actually hurting your battery life.

The Myth
"Apps in the background are running and draining my battery."
The Reality
"Background apps are frozen. Closing them forces your CPU to work twice as hard to reopen them."

How iOS Actually Works

Unlike old desktop computers, iPhones use a different logic for multitasking. When you swipe away from an app (like Instagram), iOS takes a snapshot of it and stores it in RAM.

In this state, the app is frozen. It uses:

  • Zero CPU power
  • Zero Battery
It is just sitting in the memory, waiting to be woken up.

The Cost of Re-launching

Imagine your car. Is it more fuel-efficient to turn the engine off at every red light, or to let it idle? (Okay, modern cars have start/stop, but you get the point).

When you force-close an app, you wipe it from the RAM. When you open it again 10 minutes later, your iPhone's processor has to execute a "cold start." It has to load all the data from scratch. This spike in CPU usage burns significantly more energy than simply waking a frozen app.

‚úÖ The Only Times You Should Close an App

It's Frozen/Crashed: If the app is unresponsive, kill it.
Privacy: If you don't want someone to see what was on your screen in the app switcher.
Background Audio: Apps like Spotify or Maps run in the background. Closing them actually stops a process.

Verdict

iOS is smarter than you think. Let it manage the memory. Save your thumb the effort and save your battery in the process.
