Fast Internet but Slow Steam Download? (Here is the Fix)

Feb 07, 2025 ‚Ä¢ Gaming ‚Ä¢ 3 min read

You pay for 500 Mbps internet. Speedtest.net confirms you are getting 500 Mbps. But when you try to update Cyberpunk 2077 or download a new game on Steam, it crawls at a pathetic 20 MB/s. Is your ISP throttling you? Probably not.

The "Congested Server" Problem

Steam automatically connects you to the nearest download server. If you live in New York, it connects you to the NYC server. The problem? During peak hours (evenings and weekends), millions of other people in New York are also downloading updates.

üõ†Ô∏è The "Time Zone" Hack

The secret is to connect to a server where everyone is currently asleep.

  1. Go to Steam Settings > Downloads.
  2. Look for "Download Region".
  3. If it is evening in the US, switch to London or Frankfurt (where it is early morning).
  4. If it is evening in Europe, switch to New York or Los Angeles.
  5. Restart Steam.

Why does this work?

Even though the data has to travel further (slightly higher ping), the server itself is empty. A non-congested server thousands of miles away will often push data faster than a clogged server next door.

Wait, are you reading it wrong?

Internet providers sell speeds in Megabits (Mbps). Steam shows speed in Megabytes (MB/s). They are NOT the same.

Method 2: Clear Download Cache

If changing regions didn't work, your local cache might be corrupted.

  • Go to Settings > Downloads.
  • Click "Clear Download Cache" at the bottom.
  • Steam will restart and ask you to log in again.
