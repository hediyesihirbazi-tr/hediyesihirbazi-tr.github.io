How to Clean Air Fryer Grease (Without Scrubbing)

Feb 07, 2025 ‚Ä¢ Life Hacks ‚Ä¢ 5 min read

So you cooked chicken wings (like the ones above), and now your Air Fryer basket is caked in burnt grease. Your instinct is to grab a steel wool pad and scrub it. STOP!

Air fryer baskets have a non-stick coating (Teflon or Ceramic) that is very fragile. Once you scratch it, food will stick forever, and the coating might peel into your food.

üß™ The Magic Cleaning Paste
3 tbsp Baking Soda
1 tbsp Dish Soap
White Vinegar (Spray)

The Chemical Reaction Method

1
Mix the baking soda and dish soap in a small bowl until it creates a thick paste (like toothpaste consistency).
2
Apply this paste directly onto the stubborn grease spots on the basket. Use your fingers or a soft sponge.
3
The Fun Part: Spray white vinegar over the paste. It will start to fizz and bubble. This chemical reaction lifts the burnt carbon from the surface.
4
Let it sit for 15-20 minutes. Then, simply wipe it away with warm water and a soft cloth. The grease should slide right off.

‚ö†Ô∏è Never Do This

Do NOT use cooking spray (Pam) inside your air fryer. These sprays contain lecithin, which creates a sticky, gummy residue on non-stick surfaces that is impossible to remove. Buy a reusable oil mister instead.

What about the heating element?

If the coil at the top is smoking, unplug the unit and let it cool. Turn the air fryer upside down. Use a damp cloth with a little bit of dish soap to gently wipe the coil. Never spray water directly into the heating unit.
